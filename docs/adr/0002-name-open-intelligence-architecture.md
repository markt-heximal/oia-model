# ADR-0002 — Name the model "Open Intelligence Architecture (OIA)"

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026 (v0.1)
- **Deciders:** Mark Templeton; Agentics Foundation
- **Related:** OIA Model v0.1 Decision Log

## Context

The model needed a name that would age well and carry its institutional, vendor-neutral posture.
Earlier candidates — **Sovereign Agentic Architecture (SAA)** and **SAAR** — over-committed to
moment-in-time qualifiers ("sovereign," "agentic") that risk dating the model as the field moves.

## Decision

We will name it the **Open Intelligence Architecture (OIA)**:

- **Intelligence** as a *noun*, not an adjective — the model is about intelligent systems as a class,
  not about a particular technique or moment.
- **Open** is load-bearing: vendor-neutral, institutionally published, and community-extensible.
- We reject SAA/SAAR as too tied to present-day framing.

## Consequences

- The name signals the OSI-lineage posture ("the OSI of AI") and invites contribution rather than
  consumption.
- "Open" creates an obligation the project must honor: open license ([ADR-0019](0019-license-cc-by-4.0.md)),
  open governance, and an open invitation to "push back on what does not fit."
- Avoids the trap of a name that must be revised when "agentic" or "sovereign" stops being the
  organizing theme of the field.
