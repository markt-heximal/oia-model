# About OIA Prism

> **Draft copy — to be confirmed by the author.** This About text is authored to fit the OIA body of
> work and the Prism icon; it should be reviewed against the actual application before release.

**OIA Prism** is the interactive companion to the OIA Reference Model — a way to *look through* the
architecture rather than read it linearly. Where the Reference Paper states the model and the Lens System
translates it for each audience, Prism lets you turn the same architecture toward the question in front of
you: pick a layer, a span, a threat surface, or a lens, and see how the others relate.

## What it does

- **Explore the stack.** The ten layers (0–9) and seven cross-layer spans, with each layer's purpose, key
  concerns, and open questions.
- **Switch lenses.** Read the same architecture through the CISO, CIO/CTO, or Board and Risk Lens — the
  architecture is invariant; the language changes.
- **Trace the threats.** See where the four named threat surfaces originate and propagate across the layers.
- **Follow the connections.** The State-Holding Pair, the Threat Concentration, the Verifiability Chain, and
  the Three-Actor Identity Pattern.

## About the model

The Open Intelligence Architecture is a vendor-neutral reference model for the security, sovereignty, and
governance of enterprise intelligent systems, published by the **Agentics Foundation**. It is additive to
the frameworks you already use — OSI, NIST, MITRE, OWASP, CCC, and ISO/IEC 42001 — not a replacement for
them.
